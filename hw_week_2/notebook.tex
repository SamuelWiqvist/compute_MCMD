    \paragraph{Sampling stars with masses according to the density
function}\label{sampling-stars-with-masses-according-to-the-density-function}

    \paragraph{The probability for a
supernova}\label{the-probability-for-a-supernova}

    If the cluster contains stars with masses larger than \(8 M_{\circ}\),
then these stars will explode as a supernovae. We will now compute the
probability that a cluster with \(N_{\star}=100, 300,1000\) stars
contains at least one supernova, i.e. at least on star with mass larger
than \(8 M_{\circ}\).

We now compute the probabilities by sampling clusters with
\(N_{\star}=100,300,1000\) stars using the hit-and-miss method and then
estimating the probability that we have stars in the cluster with masses
